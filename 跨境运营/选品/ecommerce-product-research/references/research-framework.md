# Research Framework

## Evidence hierarchy

1. Primary regulatory, fee, platform, supplier, and observed storefront evidence
2. Transactional or first-party performance data
3. Multiple independent market signals with aligned timing
4. Review, search, social, and community proxies
5. Single-source estimates and expert opinion
6. Unsupported assumptions

Record source, date, market, channel, observation, limitation, and decision relevance.

## Fatal screens

- The product cannot legally or operationally be sold in the target market.
- Base-case contribution margin is below the user's floor and no verified lever closes the gap.
- MOQ and lead time exceed available capital or launch timing.
- Product safety, IP, claim, or liability exposure exceeds the team's control capability.
- Demand depends on a temporary event that ends before inventory can turn.
- Differentiation is not visible or valuable at the buying decision.

## Economics

Contribution per unit equals net selling price minus landed cost, channel fees, fulfillment, expected returns, variable advertising, discounts, payment costs, and per-unit operating allowances.

Show break-even acquisition cost, break-even return rate, inventory cash cycle, and sensitivity to the two largest uncertain inputs.

## Score interpretation

Score only after fatal screens. Use 1 to 5 for demand quality, customer pain, competition accessibility, economics, sourcing, compliance, channel fit, differentiation, and evidence confidence. Keep evidence confidence separate; a high opportunity score with low confidence means validate, not proceed.
