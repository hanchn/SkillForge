# Diagnostic Method

## Validate before explaining

Confirm definition, population, grain, time boundary, filters, attribution, freshness, duplicate handling, and denominator. Recalculate from the closest trustworthy source when possible.

## Useful decompositions

- Total = population times rate
- Revenue = traffic times conversion times average order value
- Active users = acquired plus retained plus resurrected minus churned
- Mix effect = changing segment shares at stable within-segment performance
- Within effect = changing performance inside a stable segment

Use the business metric's real algebra rather than forcing a generic funnel.

## Contribution

For additive metrics, contribution is the segment's absolute delta divided by total absolute delta, with reconciliation for interacting or residual terms. For rates, show numerator and denominator and use an appropriate weighted decomposition.

## Evidence classification

- Confirmed driver: timing, exposure, mechanism, and quantified contribution align
- Contributing factor: explains part of the movement with credible evidence
- Ruled out: predicted signal is absent within an adequate test
- Unresolved: plausible but insufficiently measured
